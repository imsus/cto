# Suggested Actions Per EM Grid Cell

Reference for generating blind spot recommendations. Pick the 1–2 most relevant to the user's context.

---

## SELF × GROWTH
- Block 2–3 hours this week to build something small with an AI coding tool (Cursor, Claude Code) — even a trivial internal script counts as re-entering the technical conversation
- Pick one management book or technical talk and schedule it like a meeting (Self×Growth rarely happens unless it's on the calendar)
- Ask your manager: "Can we use our next 1:1 to talk about my own development?" — most EMs never ask this
- Spend 15 minutes writing down what you want to learn in the next quarter, then block 30 min/week for it
- Try one no-code or automation tool this week (n8n, Zapier, Bolt, Base44) — even just to see where they are

## SELF × IMPACT
- Identify one thing that only you can write or decide, that's been sitting in your head for weeks — block 90 minutes and produce it
- Turn a recurring manual task (report, update, tracking) into an automated or semi-automated tool you build yourself
- Volunteer to write the post-mortem for the last incident — it forces clarity and has org-wide impact
- Pick one business argument you've been making verbally and write it as a one-pager (saves time in every future conversation)

## SELF × CONNECTION
- Message one person you respect who works at a different company — a former colleague, someone whose content you follow — and ask for a 20-minute call
- Write one short LinkedIn post about something you've learned or observed this month
- Join one relevant online community (Slack group, Discord, newsletter community) and post something real

## PEOPLE × GROWTH
- In your next 1:1 with each direct report, ask one of: "What would you want to be doing more of?" / "What would the next level of your career look like?" / "Is there something you'd like to own that you don't own yet?"
- Pick one task you currently own that would be a stretch assignment for someone on your team — hand it off intentionally this week
- Identify who on your team you haven't advocated for recently, and write 3 bullet points about their impact (good practice for when calibration season arrives)
- Match one recurring responsibility to the engineer whose driver it fits (the impact-driven person gets the customer-facing task; the growth-driven person leads the next tech talk)
- Do a driver assessment: ask each person to take 5 minutes with the driver survey and discuss the result together

## PEOPLE × IMPACT
- Invite one engineer to silently join a stakeholder or customer call this week — frame it as "I want you to see what's on their mind"
- In the next 1:1, open with: "The feature you shipped last sprint — do you know if anyone used it?" Then share the metric
- Next time something ships, let the engineer announce it in the company channel — don't do it yourself
- Bring one concrete business number (retention, conversion, usage rate) into your next team interaction and make the connection explicit

## PEOPLE × CONNECTION
- In each 1:1 this week: ask one personal question and write down the answer somewhere you'll see before the next 1:1
- Find one engineer who had a good week and tell them specifically what you noticed — not "great job" but what specifically stood out
- Check: is anyone on your team paid below market? Even asking the question and advocating shows you care
- If someone seems off, say it out loud: "You seem a bit quieter than usual — everything ok?"

## TEAM × GROWTH
- Run the knowledge mapping exercise: list every major area of your system and ask each engineer to rate their confidence 1–3. Find the bus factor risks
- Schedule a 60-minute "AI tools day" — everyone shares one tool or use case they've tried; no judgment, just exploration
- Do a post-mortem on the last incident or major failure — not to assign blame, but to extract the lessons
- Pick one good talk from a recent conference and watch it together as a team at 1.5x speed
- Assign one engineer to lead the next retrospective — make it their meeting to own

## TEAM × IMPACT
- Try the "always green" method in the next sprint: pick one minimal, certain-to-finish goal, post it publicly, and track it daily
- After the next feature ships, schedule a 30-minute "did it land?" review two weeks out — look at usage metrics together
- List every external dependency your current sprint has — resolve or de-risk each one before committing
- Have an explicit conversation with your PM about what's the "minimal shippable" version of the next big initiative

## TEAM × CONNECTION
- Schedule one team meeting this month that has zero task updates — make it entirely about sharing, playing, or connecting
- Block a half-day team focus day in the next 6 weeks — even remote, even just 4 hours
- Ask the most connection-driven person on your team to organize the next team social activity (and give them real ownership)
- Start a "wins channel" or equivalent — a place where the team drops things they shipped or helped with, even small ones

## ORG × GROWTH
- Volunteer for one cross-team hiring panel this quarter
- Find one person in the org who is not your direct report and offer to be a sounding board for a challenge they're facing
- Contribute one concrete thing to the onboarding process or engineering standards — a document, a review, a session
- Sign up to speak at the next internal tech talk or all-hands, even just for 5 minutes

## ORG × IMPACT
- Pick one other department (support, CS, QA, finance, HR) and ask their lead: "What's the most painful thing for your team right now that we could help with?"
- Find a way to present your team's work in business terms at the next leadership meeting — not features shipped, but impact on a metric
- Ask your PM: "Is there anything our team is doing that doesn't have a business justification? Let's talk about what to cut"
- Help one stakeholder achieve one of their goals this month — something small, proactive, that they didn't ask you for

## ORG × CONNECTION
- Apply the "new person rule" starting this week: schedule one 20-minute coffee chat with someone in the company you've never spoken to
- Next time another team announces something, comment on it in Slack — genuinely, with curiosity — and follow up in DM if it sparked a question
- Go to one company-wide social event this month and actually talk to people from departments you don't normally see
- Pick 3 people in other departments whose names you know but don't really know — send them a short "I've been curious about your work" message this week
