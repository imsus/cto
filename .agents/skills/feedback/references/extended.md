# Feedback — Extended Reference

## Weekly Called Shots

A simple accountability habit that builds a lightweight feedback loop without requiring formal reviews.

Each Monday morning, have your reports send you 3–5 bullets on what they'll accomplish that week. Each Friday afternoon, they self-grade — hit or miss on each bullet, with a brief explanation for any misses.

Writing down commitments forces people to visualize what it actually takes to accomplish them. Knowing they'll need to grade themselves creates personal accountability without requiring you to chase.

The pattern also gives you signal over time:
- Someone who consistently misses their own self-assessed bullets is either over-committing or under-delivering
- Someone who always nails them may be sandbagging
- The data accumulates naturally without performance theater

This is not a micromanagement tool. It's a self-management habit for your reports that surfaces calibration problems early — before they become formal performance conversations.
