# Team Health - Sources & Further Reading

## manager.dev Articles

- [Your team meetings don't have to be boring](https://newsletter.manager.dev/p/team-meetings-dont-have-to-be-boring) - team meeting cadence and seven ideas for meeting content
- [Organizing team focus days](https://zaidesanton.substack.com/p/the-most-powerful-way-to-make-your) - Team Focus Day format and content ideas
- [Why do companies keep so many secrets](https://newsletter.manager.dev/p/why-do-companies-keep-so-many-secrets) - transparency, the "shit umbrella" anti-pattern, and the disappointment frontier
- [The 5 intensity zones of software teams](https://newsletter.manager.dev/p/the-5-intensity-zones-of-software-teams) - naming intensity zones and planning recovery after high-intensity periods

## Books

- *An Elegant Puzzle* (Will Larson) - four team health states: Falling Behind, Treading Water, Repaying Debt, Innovating
- *Peopleware: Productive Projects and Teams* (DeMarco & Lister) - jelled teams and Teamicide patterns
- *It Doesn't Have to Be Crazy at Work* (Fried & Heinemeier Hansson / Basecamp) - Trust Battery model

## External Articles

- [Google Project Aristotle](https://rework.withgoogle.com/guides/understanding-team-effectiveness/steps/introduction/) - five factors of effective teams; psychological safety as the foundation
- [Blameless PostMortems and a Just Culture](https://www.etsy.com/codeascraft/blameless-postmortems/) - Etsy Code as Craft; blameless postmortem philosophy

The Engagement Stack, activity library, and additional team-health diagnostics are original material from the manager.dev course.
