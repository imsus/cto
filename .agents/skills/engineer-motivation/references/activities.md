# Engineer Motivation — Activity Ideas by Driver (Individual Scope)

Use these when you want to give a specific engineer a stretch activity aligned with their primary driver. All activities are individual-scope — assigned to one person, not the whole team.

---

## Growth

**Assign a kingdom** — Give the engineer complete ownership of an area or app: closer communication with product, responsibility for prioritizing bugs/tech debt, reviewing PRs in that area, presenting usage stats. Making unofficial ownership official goes a long way.

**Lead a long-term tech initiative** — Something on the tech-debt side that *they* care about and that bothers them day-to-day (e.g., improving the logging infrastructure, cleaning up error standards). Support them in actually making progress on it.

**Give an internal technical talk** — Start small: 15 minutes in front of just the team, or even just one person. Topic: a trend, a complex bug they solved, a deep dive into a library. Let them choose the topic. If they enjoy it, scale up — department, whole R&D.

**Write a technical article** — Company blog or LinkedIn. Help them brainstorm the topic and with the writing process itself. Don't generate a ChatGPT article — help them write something that goes deep into a real challenge they solved.

**Lead a guild meeting** — If the company has guilds, help them prepare materials and become an active, respected member instead of just sitting and listening. If no guilds exist, a knowledge-sharing session with relevant people from other teams works too.

**Contribute to open source** — Either contribute to a library the team heavily depends on, or create a new repo in the company's GitHub for something the team actually needs.

**Sponsor a conference talk** — Help an interested engineer get approved to give an external talk. Flights/hotels are often covered by the conference, so it costs the company nothing.

**Team switching (Growth focus)** — Let the engineer spend a sprint or two on a different team that interests them professionally. They'll come back with new ideas and ways of working. Arrange with the other EM first, then offer it to the engineer — not the other way around.

---

## Connection

**Mentor a more junior engineer** — Ideally in a specific task and context, not a generic assignment. For example: a new task for the junior in an area where this engineer is strong — let them onboard, code review, and guide.

**Onboard a new engineer** — A classic for connection-driven people. You still do the 1:1s with the new engineer, but offload everything else to them.

**Lead a retrospective** — Connection-driven engineers often know what frustrated teammates better than the EM does, and are better at getting others to talk. Try an "offline" retro format: they collect input from everyone in advance, then the meeting is just discussion.

**Buddy for a newcomer** — Assign them as the go-to person for a new hire's day-to-day questions (where's lunch, how things work). Helps connect both people — but only offer this to people who actually want to do it.

**Team switching (Connection focus)** — Choose a team with great vibes, or people the engineer already likes and would want to work with.

---

## Impact

**Lead cross-department efforts** — Impact-driven engineers thrive on big-scope projects involving multiple teams, especially outside engineering. Non-technical coordination that would drain others often energizes them.

**Meet customers face-to-face** — Especially in B2B, this can be hard to arrange and might involve travel. For an impact-driven engineer, talking directly to customers is a major energy boost — worth the hassle.

**Invite to external meetings** — Business meetings, stakeholder calls, customer conversations. Ask in a 1:1 setting (no public pressure to attend). Many engineers hate these; impact-driven ones love them.

**Deeper business updates in 1:1s** — Share business context you have that most engineers don't: metrics, the sales pipeline, the real stakes of what the team is building. Do this individually with engineers who are curious about it, not as a team broadcast.

**Team switching (Impact focus)** — Especially useful if you lead a platform/DevOps/internal-facing team. Switch them to a team much closer to the customer so they can see the direct impact of their work.
